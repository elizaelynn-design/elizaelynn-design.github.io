What Is KCNA and Why Does It Matter

KCNA is an entry-level certification offered by the Cloud Native Computing Foundation (CNCF) and the Linux Foundation. It validates your understanding of Kubernetes fundamentals and the broader cloud-native ecosystem.

Key benefits

  • Recognition: Valued by top tech employers.
  • Accessibility: No prerequisites — truly beginner-friendly.
  • Foundation: Sets you up for advanced certs like CKA and CKAD.
  • Visibility: Enhances your resume, GitHub profile, and LinkedIn.

For official details and updates, visit the KCNA certification page.

KCNA Exam Format (2026 Edition)

Exam details

  • Duration: 90 minutes
  • Question type: Multiple-choice (60 questions)
  • Passing score: 75%
  • Cost: $250 USD (includes one retake)
  • Validity: 3 years
  • Delivery: Online, proctored

KCNA Syllabus Breakdown

The exam covers five domains. Here's what to expect:

  • Kubernetes Fundamentals (46%): Pods, Deployments, Services, Namespaces, YAML basics.
  • Container Orchestration (22%): Scheduling, scaling, rolling updates, cluster components.
  • Cloud Native Architecture (16%): Microservices, APIs, service meshes, loosely coupled systems.
  • Cloud Native Observability (8%): Logging, monitoring, tracing, Prometheus, Grafana.
  • Cloud Native Application Delivery (8%): CI/CD pipelines, GitOps, Helm, ArgoCD.

How to Prepare: Step-by-Step Strategy

Step 1: Learn Kubernetes basics

  • Docs: Use Kubernetes.io for official documentation.
  • Hands-on: Practice with Minikube, Kind, or Lens.
  • Core concepts: Understand Pods, ReplicaSets, Deployments, and Services.

Step 2: Explore CNCF projects

  • Helm: Package management for Kubernetes.
  • Prometheus: Metrics and monitoring.
  • ArgoCD: GitOps-based application delivery.

Step 3: Take practice exams

  • Simulate: Recreate exam conditions to build confidence.
  • Review: Study explanations for incorrect answers.
  • Track: Monitor your progress weekly.

Try free practice material: KCNA exam questions.

Step 4: Join the community

  • CNCF Slack: Ask questions and learn from practitioners.
  • GitHub discussions: Follow repositories, issues, and guides.
  • Reddit: Participate in r/kubernetes threads.

Exam-Day Tips

  • Manage time: ~1.5 minutes per question.
  • Flag and return: Don’t get stuck on a single item.
  • Stay calm: One free retake is included.
  • Use logic: Eliminate clearly wrong choices first.
